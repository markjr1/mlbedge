Recommended Bets

Algorithmic picks from 2026 standings, run differential, and matchup analysis. Each rec includes a full data breakdown and the specific game it applies to.

Note: These are algorithmic research tools based on publicly available stats — not guaranteed outcomes. Always cross-reference with starting pitcher news, injury reports, and live line movement before wagering. Bet responsibly.
How Confidence Scores Are Calculated
Every recommended bet has a confidence % — here is exactly how that number is derived for each bet type
Moneyline Value
Base: 50% (coin flip)
+gap × 200pts: Each 1% gap between Pythagorean Win% and actual Win% adds 2 confidence points
High conf: Gap ≥ 15pts → score 65–82%
Medium conf: Gap 7–15pts → score 57–65%
Cap: Maximum 82% (no bet is a certainty)
Example: Team is 5-12 but Pythagorean says 55% → gap of 19pts → confidence of 50+(19×2)=88%, capped at 82% = HIGH
Over / Under Lean
Over Lean base: 62%
Trigger: Team RS/G exceeds league avg by ≥8%
Under Lean base: 60%
Trigger: Team RA/G is ≤9% below league avg
Why not higher? O/U picks require knowledge of the specific starter ERA — the team-level trend is a lean, not a lock. Always confirm with the day's starter before betting.
These confidence scores will increase as the season grows and RS/G trends become more stable (sample size effect)
Fade (Overvalued)
Base: 57%
Trigger: W-L above .550 AND run diff below −8
Logic: Teams winning games at a rate their run differential does not support will regress — the fade capitalizes on sportsbooks still pricing them as winners
Risk: Hot teams can sustain records for 3–4 weeks before regressing — do not fade blindly; confirm they are not on a legitimate hot streak with a strong recent starter
Confidence capped at 57% because W-L overperformance can persist for multiple weeks
Important calibration note: These confidence scores are derived from historical Pythagorean regression rates across MLB. They represent the probability the bet has positive expected value, not the probability of winning any individual game. A 70% confidence pick will not win 70% of the time — it means there is strong statistical reason to believe the bet is priced incorrectly. Over a large sample (50+ picks), a well-calibrated model with 65% confidence picks should win approximately 55–58% of the time.
